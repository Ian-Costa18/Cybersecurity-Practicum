### 📘 Course Overview

- This is the Practicum Project course for CS, Public Policy, and ECE 6727.
- It's typically taken near the end of the program (after ~8 courses).
- The course focuses on applying prior knowledge to a real-world cybersecurity problem.

### 🎯 Core Structure of the Course

The course is built around three main pillars:

1. **Problem** – Define a real-world, meaningful cybersecurity problem.
2. **Solution** – Design and implement an approach to address it.
3. **Evaluation** – Prove that your solution works and is effective.

### 🧠 Project Expectations

- Projects must address a real-world or practical problem.
- You must clearly explain:
  - Why the problem matters
  - What gap exists in current solutions
  - How your solution is different or better
- The focus should be on problem definition first, not just jumping to a solution.
- Some level of novelty is required (not repeating existing work exactly).

### 🕒 Workload & Time Commitment

- This is a 5-credit course with a heavy workload.
- Expected effort:
  - ~20 hours per week
  - Over an 11-week summer term
- Requires consistent weekly progress.

### 📊 Project Development Process

Students must:

1. Define and scope a problem
2. Research existing work and identify gaps
3. Design and build a solution (technical or policy-based)
4. Evaluate results (e.g., performance, cost-benefit, usability)
5. Identify limitations and future work

### 📋 Weekly Structure & Deliverables

Every week has a deliverable:

- **Week 1:** Draft problem proposal (1–2 pages)
- **After that:** Biweekly progress reports, videos summarizing progress, peer feedback on others' work

**Grading split:**
- 50%: Final report + final presentation
- 50%: Progress reports + peer feedback

### 👥 Collaboration Rules

- Projects are individual (not group-based).
- You can have a mentor, but:
  - Work must be your own
  - Avoid dependency on others for critical progress

### 🔁 Iteration & Flexibility

- Projects may evolve over time.
- Negative or unexpected results are acceptable as long as:
  - You analyze why they occurred
  - You demonstrate learning and insight

### 💡 Project Ideas & Scope Guidance

Projects can come from:
- Workplace problems (if feasible and independent)
- Research interests

Must be:
- Feasible within ~10–11 weeks
- Neither too large (overly ambitious) nor too small

Time constraint (~225 total hours) is the key guideline for scoping.

### 🤖 Use of AI Tools

- AI tools are allowed, but:
  - Must disclose how they were used
  - Must verify outputs (e.g., references, results)

### 🧑‍🏫 Support & Learning

- Office hours and discussion forums are available.
- Peer feedback helps broaden understanding beyond your own project.
- Projects may even be expanded into research papers or portfolio work.

### ✅ Key Takeaway

This course is:
- Intensive, hands-on, and project-driven
- Focused on solving a meaningful cybersecurity problem
- Emphasizing consistent effort, critical thinking, and real-world impact
