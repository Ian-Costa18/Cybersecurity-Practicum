## 🧠 Key Guidance on Projects

### 1. Problem Statements & Evidence

- Many projects still have **"fluffy" or weakly supported problem statements**.
- Strong expectations:
  - Claims must be backed by **evidence or citations**.
  - Problem, goals, and deliverables must be **tightly aligned**.
  - Avoid unsupported claims like:
    - "This problem is important because I say so."
- Incorporate:
  - Prior research
  - Industry or academic sources
  - Examples that justify why the problem matters

### 2. Iteration & Adapting to New Information

- It's normal for new research or developments to:
  - Change your framing
  - Refine your direction
- You should integrate new findings, not restart your project.
- All reviewed projects are considered viable—build on feedback rather than beginning from scratch.

### 3. Using Literature Effectively

- Don't just list sources—synthesize them:
  - Organize key ideas
  - Highlight gaps or patterns
- Include:
  - Important supporting references
  - Evidence for claims
- Avoid:
  - Overly long literature dumps
  - Dissertation-level completeness

### 4. Methodology Expectations

- Keep methodology focused and proportional:
  - Enough rigor to justify conclusions
  - No need for overly detailed or exhaustive justification
- If findings are widely accepted (e.g., common threats), it's fine to:
  - Cite sources and move on, rather than over-analyzing

### 5. Level of Rigor & Scope

- Final deliverable should resemble a research paper, but:
  - Not expected to be publishable at top-tier venues
- Focus on:
  - Critical thinking
  - Understanding the problem space
  - Evaluating existing work
- Novel contributions are great, but not required at a high research level.

## ⏱️ Timeline & Evaluation Emphasis

- Projects must include: **Problem → Solution → Evaluation**
- Evaluation is a major component and often underestimated:
  - Should involve metrics, experiments, surveys, interviews, etc.
- Recommendations:
  - Have your solution mostly ready within ~2 weeks
  - Start reaching out to stakeholders or experts now
  - Some evaluation should appear in the final presentation, even if incomplete.

## 🤖 AI Usage Guidance

- Maintain:
  - Records of prompts
  - Documentation of how AI tools were used

## 💡 Overall Takeaways

- Projects are generally on track, mostly in the B range (5.5–6.5 / 7).

  - Evidence-backed arguments
  - Clear alignment between problem and solution
  - Thoughtful synthesis of literature
  - Early planning for evaluation

✅ **Bottom line:** Stay focused, iterate with feedback, support your claims with evidence, and prioritize evaluation as you move into the next phase.
