# Week 3 (June 1st) Office Hours Summary

*Posted by Blain A Checkley (Staff)*

> Note: Office hours were not held on May 25th due to the Memorial Day holiday. This summary covers the June 1st session.

---

### 📌 Course Status & Upcoming Tasks

- The session marks the start of Week 3 of the practicum.
- Students have submitted the first progress report (including timeline and plan).
- **Upcoming deliverables:**
  - Project introduction video (due early in the week)
  - Peer feedback report (due Sunday)

### 🎥 Project Video Expectations

The video should:
- Clearly explain:
  - The problem being addressed
  - Motivation behind the project
  - Initial solution approach
- Highlight areas where feedback is needed.
- **Purpose:** Gather peer feedback and learn about others' projects and problems

### 🤝 Peer Feedback Guidelines

Feedback must be:
- Constructive
- Actionable
- Informed (based on understanding and research)

Avoid:
- Generic comments like "great job" (not meaningful)

Expectations:
- Review at least ~4 peer videos
- Demonstrate understanding of the problem and proposed solution
- Offer thoughtful critique and suggestions

### 📝 Common Issues in First Drafts

#### 1. Problem Definition
- Many students did not clearly define the exact problem.
- **Expectation:** Provide a concise, explicit problem statement; clearly scope what you will (and won't) do.

#### 2. "What" vs. "How"
- Students often explained *what* they want to do, but not *how* they will do it.
- **Expectation:** Include methods, tasks or steps, and approach for implementation.

#### 3. Background Research
- Projects should be informed by existing work, not based on unsupported claims.
- Must include: related work, explanation of the gap your project fills, evidence or citations to support claims.

#### 4. Evaluation Plan
- Many proposals lacked a clear plan to evaluate results.
- **Expectation:** Show how you will validate your solution; support claims with evidence or analysis.

### 📊 Evaluation Approaches (Especially for Policy Projects)

**Strong methods:**
- Stakeholder feedback (highly recommended)
- Interviews with subject matter experts and real-world stakeholders

**Weaker method:**
- Retrospective analysis ("what would have happened") – considered less reliable due to bias

### 🧠 Stakeholder Engagement Tips

- Prefer qualitative interviews over surveys (more depth)
- Start early – identifying stakeholders and arranging discussions takes time
- Document notes and key insights (not necessarily full transcripts)

### 📐 Project Scope & Depth

**Key principle:** ✅ Depth over breadth; ❌ Avoid overly broad projects

Recommendations:
- Focus narrowly on a problem
- Provide detailed analysis and concrete examples
- Use appendices for technical depth if needed

### 🧪 Policy Project Guidance

Include:
- Clear threat model
- Context of who/what is impacted

Structure:
- High-level policy in main body
- Technical controls can go in appendix if too detailed

### ⚙️ Project Planning & Execution

- Break project into small, realistic weekly steps
- Benefits: better time management, clearer progress tracking

### ⏱️ Time Expectations

- Estimated effort: ~20–25 hours per week (summer schedule estimate)
- Grading is based on output and progress, not detailed time tracking

### ✅ Grading & Feedback

- Early work (proposal, first reports): graded by instructors
- Later reports: graded by TAs
- Progress reports should show substantive development over time

### 💡 Key Takeaways

- Clearly define and scope your problem
- Explain how you will solve it (not just what)
- Support your work with research and evidence
- Plan a meaningful evaluation strategy
- Provide thoughtful peer feedback
- Focus on depth and concreteness
- Use stakeholder input wherever possible
