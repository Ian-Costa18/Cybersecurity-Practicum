### 🧾 Peer Feedback Expectations

**What good feedback looks like:**
- Constructive – helps improve the project
- Specific and actionable
- Based on an actual understanding of the project
- May include relevant references or suggestions

**Using AI:**
- Allowed, but must verify accuracy and ensure relevance
- Avoid: generic responses, incorrect or hallucinated information

> 👉 **Key idea:** Put effort into understanding others' work, not just your own.

### 💡 Project Development Guidance

**Define your project clearly:**
- Who is your target user or organization?
- What are their specific challenges and constraints?
- What value does your solution provide?

**Make it practical:**
- Base your work on real-world scenarios
- Use examples or case studies
- Ensure your solution is actionable

**Demonstration:**
- Show how your solution works
- Use examples, simulated or retrospective cases

### 🎯 Scope Management (Critical)

A major challenge across projects is scope being too broad.

**Best practices:**
- Narrow down the problem – focus on a specific use case or sector
- Use threat models and clearly defined boundaries
- Avoid trying to solve everything

> 👉 **Key idea:** Smaller, well-defined projects are more effective than large, vague ones.

### 🔍 Research & Problem-Solving Process

Research is:
- Non-linear
- Iterative and sometimes messy

Expect: dead ends, changes in direction, uncertainty

**Strategies:**
- Set a time budget for difficult problems
- If stuck: pivot to other approaches
- Document failed attempts and what you learned

> 👉 **Key idea:** "Failed" attempts still provide valuable insights.

### 🔁 Progress Reports

Should demonstrate:
- Consistent effort
- Progress over time

It's okay if tasks are completed out of order or plans change.

Include:
- What you planned vs. what you did
- Why things changed

**Important mindset:** Progress reports are not just assignments – they are building blocks for your final report.

### 🛠️ Practical Tips from Q&A

**Project execution:**
- Ground your project in a specific domain and a realistic scenario
- For AI/security projects: focus on specific attack types and clear threat models
- Try to demonstrate attacks and explore defenses (even conceptually)

**Technical challenges:**
- If computational resources are needed: ask in forums or check available academic tools

### ⚠️ Common Challenges & Advice

- **Scope issues:** Narrow your focus early
- **Technical roadblocks:** Set limits on time spent; be willing to pivot
- **Feeling overwhelmed:** This is normal – everyone experiences uncertainty in research

**Key mindset:** Learning > perfection; Progress > completeness

### ✅ Overall Takeaways

- Be clear and focused in your project
