# Final Report Assignment

> **⚠️ HARD CONSTRAINT — CS students: the final report body must not exceed 12 pages, excluding references.**

## Task

Submit a final report that fully documents your project, including:

1. The **motivation** for the problem,
2. **Details of the solution**, and
3. A **discussion of its efficacy and limitations**.

Make sure the report is well researched, that any claims are fully supported, and that practical applicability is clearly articulated. Include an appendix with the technical project, such as a code base, or policy proposal, such as draft language, if appropriate.

## Report Length Guidelines

- Reports typically range from **10 to 15 pages**.
- **CS and ECE Students: the final report should not exceed 12 pages, excluding references.** ← applies to me
- PUBP Students: the final report should not exceed 20 pages, not including appendices.

Appendices may be included as needed, but students should ensure that **all essential material for evaluation appears within the main body of the report.**

## Format

- Encouraged (not required) to use an existing research-paper style template: **IEEE, ACM, or JoynerDF**.
- Provide **in-line citations** where necessary.
- Include **end references** in an accepted format such as **IEEE or APA**.
- Include a **title** and an **abstract**, as scientific papers do.

## Final Presentation and Report Reminder to Our Summer 2026 Cyber Jackets

### Final report structure

Always follow the Final Project Report assignment first. The outline below is a general suggestion, not a required template. Projects differ, so technical, policy, and research-focused reports may organize their content differently. Use your judgment and creativity to combine, rename, or reorder sections when doing so improves the report, while still addressing all assignment requirements.

A typical report may include:

- Title and abstract
- Introduction and motivation
- The cybersecurity problem and why it matters
- Background or related research
- Project objectives or requirements
- Proposed solution
- Methodology, design, and implementation
- Evaluation approach
- Results and findings
- Discussion of the solution's efficacy
- Practical applicability and real-world deployment
- Limitations and assumptions
- Future work
- Conclusion
- References
- Appendix with the technical or policy work product

### Make the major ideas easy to find

The exact headings and organization are up to you. However, readers should be able to clearly identify the cybersecurity problem, the proposed solution, the evaluation, results and findings, practical applicability, limitations, and appendix work product.

Some of these elements may be combined or placed within broader sections, depending on the nature of the project. A polished introduction is useful, but it should still clearly explain the problem, the gap, why it matters, and what the project contributes.

### Evidence and evaluation

Support claims with appropriate research, evidence, and inline citations.
Clearly present the evaluation, results, and findings in the main report.
Essential material needed to evaluate the project should not appear only in the appendix.

### Writing and formatting

- Follow standard academic writing practices. Organization, writing quality, citations, and presentation all matter.
- Include a title and abstract.
- Use inline citations and a consistent reference format such as IEEE or APA.
- Reports typically range from 10 to 15 pages, subject to the limits below.
- CS and ECE reports should not exceed 12 pages, excluding references.
- PUBP reports should not exceed 20 pages, excluding appendices.
- IEEE, ACM, or JoynerDF templates are encouraged but not required.
